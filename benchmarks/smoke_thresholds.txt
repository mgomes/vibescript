# Benchmark smoke thresholds for CI guardrails.
# Format:
# <benchmark_name> <max_ns_per_op> <max_allocs_per_op> [max_bytes_per_op]
#
# Enforced by scripts/bench_smoke_check.sh, which CI runs on every pull
# request and push to master (the "Benchmark smoke gates" step in
# .github/workflows/benchmarks.yml). A breach fails the workflow.
#
# Keep thresholds intentionally loose enough to avoid platform flake,
# but tight enough to catch obvious regressions.

BenchmarkExecutionArithmeticLoop 5000000 1000
BenchmarkExecutionStepLoop 16000000 100 10000
BenchmarkExecutionArrayPipeline 40000000 20000
# Allocation-heavy accumulation benchmarks: ns/op is GC- and shared-runner-
# variance-sensitive, so the prior 3.0M/3.5M ns/op caps sat just below the CI
# runner's actual best-of-1 (~3.0M and ~3.57M) and tripped intermittently on
# unrelated PRs. Widen the ns/op headroom to absorb runner variance; the alloc
# and byte caps (the meaningful, stable signals here) are unchanged.
BenchmarkExecutionArrayPushAccumulation 3600000 10000 800000
BenchmarkExecutionArrayConcatAccumulation 4200000 12000 900000
BenchmarkExecutionMethodDispatchLoop 8000000 1000 130000
BenchmarkBuiltinMemberDispatchLoop 5000000 100 10000

# ns/op headroom to absorb runner variance while still catching gross
# regressions; the alloc/byte caps (the meaningful, stable signals here) are
# unchanged.
